Purple Side (Hybrid)

The Purple Side, also known as Hybrid Security, combines both Offensive (Red Team) and Defensive (Blue Team) security strategies to create a more comprehensive security posture. Purple Team members work to bridge the gap between attack simulation and defense mechanisms, ensuring that the insights gained from offensive testing are effectively used to improve defensive measures. Their goal is to enhance collaboration between the Red and Blue Teams, promoting continuous improvement in an organization's overall security strategy through a feedback loop of simulated attacks and defensive improvements.

Threat Intelligence Analyst

Threat Intelligence Analysts focus on gathering, analyzing, and interpreting information about current and emerging cyber threats. They monitor various sources of threat data, including open-source intelligence (OSINT), dark web forums, and threat feeds, to identify potential risks. By understanding the tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) of threat actors, they provide actionable intelligence to improve an organization's security posture. Their work is essential in anticipating and mitigating cyber threats before they can cause significant harm.

Courses available:

  • Threat Intelligence Basics

    Introduction to the fundamentals of threat intelligence, including data collection, analysis, and dissemination.

  • Threat Analysis and Attribution

    Methods for analyzing threat actor behaviors and attributing cyber attacks to specific groups or individuals.

Malware Analyst

Malware Analysts specialize in examining and understanding malicious software to determine its purpose, functionality, and potential impact. They use reverse engineering techniques to dissect malware, uncovering its behavior and identifying indicators of compromise (IOCs). Malware Analysts provide detailed reports on their findings, helping organizations to develop effective countermeasures and improve their incident response capabilities. Their expertise is crucial in understanding and mitigating the threats posed by various types of malware, including viruses, worms, trojans, and ransomware.

Courses available:

  • Malware Analysis Fundamentals

    Introduction to the basics of malware analysis, including static and dynamic analysis techniques.

  • Reverse Engineering Malware

    Advanced techniques for reverse engineering malware to understand its functionality and behavior.

Security Consultant

Security Consultants provide expert advice and guidance to organizations on how to protect their information assets. They conduct security assessments, develop security policies, and recommend best practices to mitigate risks. Security Consultants often work on a project basis, helping organizations to implement security measures and comply with industry standards and regulations. Their role is crucial in identifying security gaps and developing strategies to address them, ensuring that organizations can protect themselves against cyber threats effectively.

Courses available:

  • Security Consulting Fundamentals

    Introduction to the principles and practices of security consulting, including risk assessment and mitigation strategies.

  • Advanced Security Assessments

    Techniques for conducting thorough security assessments and developing detailed recommendations for improvement.

Cybersecurity Auditor

Cybersecurity Auditors are responsible for evaluating an organization's security controls and processes to ensure compliance with policies, standards, and regulations. They conduct audits to assess the effectiveness of security measures, identify weaknesses, and recommend improvements. Cybersecurity Auditors play a vital role in maintaining an organization's security posture by providing independent reviews and ensuring that security practices align with industry best practices and regulatory requirements. Their work helps to identify areas for improvement and supports continuous security enhancement efforts.

Courses available:

  • Cybersecurity Audit Fundamentals

    Basics of conducting cybersecurity audits, including audit planning, execution, and reporting.

  • Advanced Cybersecurity Auditing

    Advanced techniques for auditing complex IT environments and evaluating security controls.
